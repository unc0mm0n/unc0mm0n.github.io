My thesis focused on the introduction of a novel parallel Single-Pair-Shortest-Path algorithm. During that time I took a number of student positions, adding to my experience in high performance development in different environments, and receiving an introduction to cloud computing and large scale app deployment.

Between July 2016 and January 2019 I worked as an interim software engineer at Qualcomm. The position started with a focus on Python CI and automation, involving automation of testing across a cluster of computers and hardware using Jenkins at first and followed by a creation of a fullstack in-house automation system using Flask, a few MySQL databases and Redis. Later I moved into the embedded software development team, where we worked on L1 of Qualcomm's first 5G cellular Modem! This challenging position involved writing high performance real-time C code working in parallel on multiple CPUs (and some DSPs) and reading and understanding 3gpp's series 38 spec.

I finished my Bachelors in Mathematics and Computer Science at Tel Aviv University with a GPA of 88 in July 2018. Gaining solid foundations in Mathematics before taking a deep dive into some of the more interesting subjects of computer science, taking courses in Algorithms, Data Structures, Computer structure and Operating Systems, and Computer Graphics and Image processing.

In my free time I'm an avid chess and board-games player, always enjoy activities that require me to think. I also take this time to study new ideas and test my abilities.
